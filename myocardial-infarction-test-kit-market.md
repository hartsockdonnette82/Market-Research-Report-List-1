Plasma samples, which contain clotting factors, offer a comprehensive view of biochemical changes post-infarction. Whole blood samples, the simplest form, allow for rapid testing and immediate results, making them ideal for emergency settings. Each sample type plays a crucial role in diagnosing and managing myocardial infarction effectively.</p></p>
