<p><p>The Positron Emission Tomography (PET) system market is categorized into three types: direct imaging, indirect imaging, and alternative imaging. Direct imaging employs scintillation crystals to capture gamma rays emitted during positron annihilation, providing high-resolution images. Indirect imaging uses a combination of scintillators and photomultiplier tubes to convert gamma rays into visible light for imaging. Alternative imaging encompasses emerging technologies like hybrid PET/MRI systems and other novel modalities that enhance diagnostic capabilities and provide additional functional information. Each type serves distinct diagnostic needs in medical imaging.</p></p>
