<p><ul><li>Carbide-base</li><li>Oxide-base</li><li>Nitride-based</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The cermet market is segmented into carbide-base, oxide-base, and nitride-base types. Carbide-based cermets, primarily composed of tungsten carbide, are known for their high hardness and wear resistance, making them ideal for cutting tools. Oxide-based cermets, featuring materials like zirconia, offer superior thermal stability and corrosion resistance, often used in high-temperature applications. Nitride-based cermets, primarily involving titanium nitride, provide excellent abrasion resistance and are commonly used in aerospace and automotive industries for demanding environments.</p></p>
