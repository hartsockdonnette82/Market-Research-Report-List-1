<p><p>The bicycle derailleur market is primarily divided into two main segments: front derailleurs and rear derailleurs. Front derailleurs manage the gear shifts between the chainrings at the front of the bike, playing a crucial role in changing gears for various terrains. Rear derailleurs, on the other hand, control the shifting of the chain across the sprockets on the rear cassette. Both types are essential for optimizing gearing performance, enhancing rider experience, and adapting to diverse riding conditions.</p></p>
