<p><p>The machining aluminum market encompasses various processes, including turning and milling, each serving distinct applications. Turning machining involves rotating the aluminum workpiece against a stationary cutting tool to create cylindrical parts, ideal for components like shafts and fittings. Milling machining, on the other hand, employs rotating cutters to remove material from a stationary workpiece, suitable for producing complex shapes and features. Additionally, "others" includes processes like drilling and electrical discharge machining, addressing specialized needs within the aluminum sector.</p></p>
