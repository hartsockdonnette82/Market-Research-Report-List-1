<p><p>Data entry software can be categorized into two primary market types: on-premises and cloud-based. On-premises software is installed locally on a company’s hardware, providing greater control over data and security but requiring more maintenance and upfront costs. In contrast, cloud-based software is hosted on remote servers, accessible via the internet, offering flexibility and scalability. This model often reduces IT overhead and allows for easier collaboration, but may raise concerns about data privacy and reliance on internet connectivity.</p></p>
