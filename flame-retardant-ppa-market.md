<p><p>The Flame Retardant PPA (polyphthalamide) market is primarily divided into two types: halogen-type and halogen-free type. Halogen-type flame retardants incorporate elements like chlorine or bromine to enhance fire resistance but raise environmental and safety concerns. Halogen-free types, often based on phosphorus or mineral components, provide effective flame protection while minimizing toxic emissions during combustion. The increasing regulatory focus on environmental sustainability is driving a shift towards halogen-free options, appealing to industries prioritizing safety and eco-friendliness.</p></p>
