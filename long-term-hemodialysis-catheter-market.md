<p><p>The Long-Term Hemodialysis Catheter market comprises two primary types: silicone and polyurethane. Silicone catheters are favored for their flexibility, reduced risk of thrombosis, and biocompatibility, making them suitable for extended use. In contrast, polyurethane catheters offer greater strength and durability, which can lead to longer lifespan and less frequent replacements. Both materials cater to different patient needs, influencing their adoption rates in clinical settings, as healthcare providers seek to optimize patient care and minimize complications associated with dialysis.</p></p>
