<p><p>Utility microgrids can be categorized into three main types: AC microgrid systems, which primarily use alternating current to integrate with the larger grid and support conventional loads; DC microgrid systems, designed for applications with direct current needs, ideal for renewable energy sources and electronic devices; and hybrid microgrid systems, which combine both AC and DC capabilities, offering flexibility and efficiency. These systems facilitate energy resilience, support local generation, and enhance grid reliability while catering to diverse energy demands.</p></p>
