<p><ul><li>Natural Type Colloids (Blood Plasma)</li><li>Synthetic Type Colloids (Blood Plasma)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Colloids in the blood plasma market are fluid substances that contain particles dispersed uniformly throughout a liquid. Natural type colloids, like human albumin and fresh frozen plasma, are derived from biological sources and are used for volume replacement in clinical settings. Synthetic type colloids, such as hydroxyethyl starch and dextran, are manufactured to mimic the properties of natural colloids and are utilized in medical treatments to manage fluid balance and support blood volume during surgeries or trauma.</p></p>
