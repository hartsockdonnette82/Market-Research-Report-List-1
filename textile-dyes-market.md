<p><p>The textile dyes market encompasses various types, including disperse dyes, reactive dyes, sulfur dyes, vat dyes, and acid dyes. Disperse dyes are primarily used for synthetic fibers, offering vibrant colors. Reactive dyes bond chemically with fabrics, ideal for cotton. Sulfur dyes provide deep, rich colors, suitable for dark shades. Vat dyes are known for their colorfastness and are used on cellulose fibers. Acid dyes, used predominantly on wool and nylon, offer bright colors and good solubility. Other dyes include pigments and natural dyes, catering to diverse applications.</p></p>
