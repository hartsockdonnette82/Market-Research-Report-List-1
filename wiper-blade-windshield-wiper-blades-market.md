<p><p>Wiper blades, essential for clear visibility during rain or snow, come in two main types: boneless and bone wiper blades. Boneless wiper blades are sleek, frameless designs that use a single, flexible rubber strip for even pressure distribution, reducing streaking and noise. Bone wiper blades, also known as traditional or conventional wiper blades, feature a metal frame with multiple pressure points. Both types are widely used in the automotive market, with boneless blades gaining popularity for their durability and aerodynamic performance, while bone blades remain cost-effective and reliable.</p></p>
