<p><p>The automotive cooling systems market includes two primary types: liquid-cooled and air-cooled systems. Liquid-cooled systems use a coolant fluid circulated through the engine to absorb and dissipate heat via a radiator. They are efficient, suitable for high-performance vehicles, and maintain consistent engine temperatures. Air-cooled systems rely on airflow over fins or surfaces to cool the engine, offering simplicity and lower maintenance but are less effective for high heat loads. Both systems aim to regulate engine temperature, ensuring optimal performance and longevity.</p></p>
