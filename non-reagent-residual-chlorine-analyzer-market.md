<p><ul><li>Colorimetric Measurement</li><li>Polarographic Measurement</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Non-reagent Residual Chlorine Analyzer market includes two primary measurement types: colorimetric and polarographic. Colorimetric measurement involves using light absorption to detect chlorine levels, providing accurate readings through color changes in a sample. In contrast, polarographic measurement relies on electrochemical principles, where chlorine interacts with a sensor to produce a measurable current. Both methods offer efficient, reagent-free solutions for monitoring residual chlorine in water treatment processes, enhancing water quality management without the need for chemical reagents.</p></p>
