<p><p>A Gigabit Interface Converter (GBIC) is a hot-swappable network interface used to connect Gigabit Ethernet transceivers to various networking equipment. The market for GBICs includes two primary types: single-mode GBICs, which are designed for long-distance communication over fiber optic cables, allowing signals to travel over several kilometers; and multimode GBICs, which are suited for shorter distances and use multiple light paths within the fiber. Both types cater to different networking needs and infrastructure requirements.</p></p>
