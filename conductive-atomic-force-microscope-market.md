<p><p>The conductive atomic force microscope (CAFM) market is divided into two main types: contact type and contactless type. </p><p>The contact type involves the probe making physical contact with the sample surface, allowing for precise measurements of conductivity and surface properties. In contrast, the contactless type operates without direct contact, utilizing techniques like scanning tunneling microscopy to gather data while minimizing sample damage. Both types are essential for applications in materials science, semiconductor research, and nanotechnology, providing valuable insights into electrical properties at the nanoscale.</p></p>
